- Frequencies are allocated as __ranges__. Ranges are then split into __channels__ (smaller ranges).
- If the demand for channel is too high, that channel can become __oversaturated__. It degrades the quality of communication.
---
___Saturation Techniques___
- __DSSS (Direct-Sequence Spread Spectrum)__
	- Designed to spread a signal over a larger frequency band.
	- Used by 802.11b devices.
	- 2.4 GHz frequency.
- __FHSS (Frequency-Hopping Spread Spectrum)__
	- Relies on spread spectrum methods to communicate.
	- Transmits signals by rapidly switching a carrier signal among many channels.
	- __sender__ and __receiver__ must be synchronized to __"know"__.
	- Used by original 802.11
	- __Walkie-talkie__, __Bluetooth__.
- __OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing)__
	- Single channel uses multiple __sub-channels__ on adjacent frequencies.
	- Used by 802.11a/g/n/ac
---
## ___Channel Selection___
- Use __non-overlapping channels__
- __2.4 GHz__  
	- allotted 22MHz bandwidth, separated by 5 MHz.
	- 802.11b/g/n
	- 11 channels for North America (13 for Europe, 14 for Japan)
- __5 GHz__
	- 802.11a/n/ac
	- 24 channels, separated by 20 MHz.
	- Three sections
---
## ___Plan a WLAN Deployment___
- ___Recommendations:___
	- If APs are to use existing wiring or can be placed somewhere, note this on the map.
	- Position APs above obstructions
	- Note all potential sources of interfaces.
	- Position APs vertically near the ceiling in the center of each coverage area.
	- Position APs in locations where users are expected to be.
