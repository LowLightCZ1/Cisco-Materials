- Enables a __WLC__ to manage multiple APs and WLANs.
- Encapsulate and forward WLAN client traffic between an AP and WLC
- Based on __LWAPP__.
- Adds additional security with __DTLS (Datagram Transport Layer Security)__.
- Operates over IPv4 or IPv6 (IPv4 default).
---
- Use UPD ports __5246__ and __5247__.
- Port __5246__ - control messages used by the WLC to manage the AP.
- Port __5247__ - Encapsuling data packets travelling to and from clients.
---
- IPv4 uses IP protocol 17
- IPv6 uses IP protocol 136
---
## ___Split MAC Architecture___
- Split _media access control_.
- ___Components___
	- AP MAC Functions
	- WLC MAC Functions

| AP MAC Functions                             | WLC MAC Functions                                    |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Beacons and probe responses                  | Authentication                                       |
| Packet Acknowledgements and restransmissions | Association and re-association of roaming client     |
| Frame queueing and packet priorization       | Frame translation to other protocols                 |
| MAC layer data encryption and decryption     |  Termination of 802.11 traffic on a wired interface  |
___
## ___DTLS Encryption___
- Protocol which provides security between AP and the WLC.
- Prevents eavesdropping or tampering.
- Enabled by default. Disabled for the data channel. 
---
## ___FlexConnect APs___
- Wireless solution for a brach office and remote office deployments.
- Lets you configure and control access point in a branch office trough a WAN link.
- ___Modes:___
	- __Connected Mode__ - WLC is reachable. AP has CAPWAP connectivity with the WLC.
	- __Standalone Mode__ - WLC is unreachable. AP can assume some of the WLC functions locally.
